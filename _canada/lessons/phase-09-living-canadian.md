# Phase 9: Living Canadian

Phases 1–8 taught you Canada the way a very good book does. This phase
teaches what the book can't: **the culture you'd have absorbed by
osmosis if you'd grown up there** — the school gym on a February
morning, the exact register of "sorry," what a double-double is and
when to order one, why the whole street goes quiet for an overtime
goal, and the hundred small competencies natives never think to
explain because they never learned them either; they just *were* them.

It's written for exactly you: someone not born or raised in Canada who
wants to know how the place feels from the inside — the childhood, the
unwritten rules, the daily textures, the winter, the food, the hockey,
the words. It comes *after* the Lesson 47 capstone deliberately: first
the exam, then the life.

Every lesson keeps the track's format — source & scenario labs with
hidden model answers, factual checkpoints (now culture-flavoured), and
homework you can do in an actual Canadian week.

# Phase 1: The Land

You can't understand Canada's history, politics, or economy without its
geography — the country is, before anything else, an argument with distance
and winter. This phase builds the mental map everything else hangs on: the
thirteen provinces and territories, the seven physical regions, the climate
bands and the idea of "the North," the thin ribbon where people actually
live, and the resources that made (and still make) the economy.

Every lesson's lab is a **source & scenario exercise** — a map, data table, or
primary source to work with, plus a realistic scenario — and the checkpoints
are citizenship-test-style questions, so this phase doubles as prep for the
geography section of the Canadian citizenship test.

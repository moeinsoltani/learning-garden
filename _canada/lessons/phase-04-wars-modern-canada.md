# Phase 4: The World Wars to Modern Canada

The century in which Canada became fully independent, fully urban, and had
to decide — twice — whether it would remain one country. From Vimy Ridge
and the conscription crisis, through the Depression and the Second World
War, the postwar boom that built medicare and the flag, Quebec's Quiet
Revolution and two referendums, the 1982 Constitution and Charter, to the
reckoning with residential schools and the Canada of today.

Every lesson's lab is a **source & scenario exercise** with hidden model
answers; checkpoints are citizenship-test-style questions.

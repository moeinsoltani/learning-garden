# Phase 2: First Peoples & the Colonial Era

The deep history: the nations who lived here for millennia, the Europeans who
arrived to fish and stayed to trade, the French society planted on the
St. Lawrence, the fur-trade economy that mapped a continent, and the British
conquest whose accommodation of French Canada — the Quebec Act bargain — still
structures the country. The phase ends with the American Revolution's
refugees creating English Canada, and the War of 1812 keeping it separate.

Every lesson's lab is a **source & scenario exercise** — primary-source
excerpts, maps, and "explain it to a friend" scenarios with hidden model
answers — and checkpoints are citizenship-test-style questions.

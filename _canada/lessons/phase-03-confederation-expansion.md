# Phase 3: Confederation & Expansion

How four colonies became a transcontinental country in barely two decades —
and at whose expense. This phase runs from the 1837 rebellions and the
winning of responsible government, through the Confederation bargain of
1867, the railway that stitched on the West, the Métis resistances of Louis
Riel, the peopling of the Prairies — and the treaties, the Indian Act, and
the residential schools that were nation-building's other face.

Every lesson's lab is a **source & scenario exercise** with hidden model
answers; checkpoints are citizenship-test-style questions.

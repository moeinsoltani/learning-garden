# Phase 9: Business & Product Thinking

A senior engineer thinks about how to build things well; a lead thinks about *what* to build and *why* —
which requires understanding the business and product context that should drive engineering decisions.
This phase develops that broader thinking: how your company makes money, the product strategy your PM
does, metrics (business and engineering) and their honest use, connecting technical decisions to
business outcomes, and the lead's constant question — "should we build this at all?" Thinking in
business and product terms is what makes you a partner in deciding direction, not just an executor of it.

The labs are scenario exercises — you analyze your company's business model, argue a roadmap, respond to
a "prove productivity" request, build a business case, and decide whether to kill a project. Business and
product thinking is what elevates a technical leader into a business leader.

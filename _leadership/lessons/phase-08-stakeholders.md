# Phase 8: Stakeholder Management

Much of a lead's work flows through relationships with the people around them — product managers and
designers, your own manager, customers and users, other engineering teams, and leadership. Managing
these stakeholders well is what lets you ship the right thing, get support and resources, keep the
team connected to reality, and unblock dependencies. This phase builds those skills: forming a
healthy triad with PMs and designers, managing up so your manager is effective on your behalf,
keeping the team connected to customers, getting things from teams that don't work for you, and
negotiating scope, time, and staffing.

The labs are scenario exercises — you push back on a PM, warn your director of a slip, navigate a
customer escalation, get an API from a busy platform team, and run a scope negotiation. Stakeholder
relationships are the channels through which much of your impact happens.

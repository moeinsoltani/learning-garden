# Phase 11: People Management (the EM path)

This phase covers the people-management responsibilities that come specifically with becoming an
Engineering Manager — the formal duties of managing people, not just leading projects. If you're
heading toward the EM path (vs. the tech-lead/staff path), these are the core skills: evaluating
performance fairly, handling underperformance, hiring and interviewing, evaluating and closing
candidates, understanding motivation, designing team structures, and building psychological safety.

Even on the tech-lead path, understanding these makes you a better partner to managers and a stronger
leader. The labs are scenario exercises — you write a hard review, plan a PIP arc, design an interview
loop, run a debrief, diagnose motivation, design a team split, and build a culture plan. Managing people
well is a profound responsibility and a distinct craft.

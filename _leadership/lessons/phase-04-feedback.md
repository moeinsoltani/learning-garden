# Phase 4: Feedback & Difficult Conversations

Giving feedback, recognizing good work, receiving criticism gracefully, and navigating
high-stakes conversations are among the most consequential — and most avoided — things a
lead does. Handled well, they build trust, change behavior, and keep a team healthy; handled
poorly (or ducked), they let problems fester and erode relationships. This phase builds the
core skills: behavioral feedback that lands (SBI), recognition that motivates, receiving
feedback so people keep telling you the truth, staying effective in crucial conversations,
and defusing emotional situations.

The labs are scenario exercises — realistic hard moments where you write your actual words,
then compare against a model answer with the reasoning. It pairs with the
[English for Work]({{ '/english/learning-plan.html' | relative_url }}) track's Phase 8
(Feedback & Hard Conversations), which teaches the *language*; this phase teaches the
*judgment*.

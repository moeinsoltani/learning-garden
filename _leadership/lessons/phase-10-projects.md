# Phase 10: Project Leadership

Once you've decided what's worth building (Phase 9), you have to actually deliver it — and leading
projects to successful completion is a distinct, learnable craft. This phase covers the execution side:
planning so the plan survives reality, estimating honestly and communicating uncertainty, finding the
risks that will actually kill a project, managing dependencies, prioritizing and saying no while staying
trusted, recovering slipping projects without death marches, and leading during incidents and
postmortems.

The labs are scenario exercises — you build milestone plans, repair broken estimate-communication,
run pre-mortems, design dependency coordination, re-prioritize under pressure, write recovery plans, and
run blameless postmortems. Delivering projects reliably is much of what a lead is judged on.

# Phase 2: The First Trimester

The first thirteen weeks: the pregnancy is confirmed, the baby's major organs
form, and the mother's body changes fast — often with nausea and exhaustion, and
often before anyone else knows. This phase covers confirming the pregnancy and
starting prenatal care, coping with early symptoms, eating and living safely, the
early screening choices, and — honestly — the reality of early loss and how to
support each other through it.

{: .important }
> General education, not medical advice — always follow your own doctor, OB, or
> midwife. When in doubt, call.

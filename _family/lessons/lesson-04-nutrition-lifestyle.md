# Lesson 04: Nutrition & Lifestyle Before Pregnancy

{: .important }
> General education, not medical advice. Ask your provider about doses and your
> own situation — especially the right folic acid dose for you.

{: .note }
> **Words to know**
> - **folic acid / folate** — a B-vitamin that helps prevent serious defects of the baby's brain and spine (neural-tube defects).
> - **neural-tube defect** (NEW-ral) — a birth defect of the spine/brain (e.g. spina bifida) that forms in the first weeks.
> - **prenatal vitamin** — a daily multivitamin made for pregnancy (includes folic acid, iron, and more).
> - **BMI** (Body Mass Index) — a rough measure of weight relative to height.
> - **sperm** — the male reproductive cells; they take about 3 months to mature.

## Concept

Before pregnancy, you're preparing the *environment* a baby will grow in — and two
people contribute to it. The headline is **folic acid**: taken before conception,
it dramatically lowers the risk of serious spine and brain defects that form in
the **first few weeks**, often before a woman knows she's pregnant. That timing is
why it's a *before* task, not an *after* one.

Around that headline sits a simple, powerful set of habits — good food, a healthy
weight, no smoking or alcohol, movement, sleep, less stress — that improve
fertility and give the pregnancy the best possible start. And crucially, **the
partner's habits matter too**: sperm take ~3 months to mature, so changes made
now pay off.

## Going Deeper

### Folic acid — the one supplement to start now

- **Most people:** **0.4 mg (400 mcg) daily**, ideally starting **2–3 months
  before** conception and continuing through at least the first trimester.
- **Higher dose (e.g. 1 mg up to 4–5 mg):** for higher-risk situations — a
  previous pregnancy with a neural-tube defect, diabetes, certain medications
  (e.g. some epilepsy drugs), or higher body weight — **only on medical advice.**
- A **prenatal vitamin** is an easy way to get folic acid plus iron, vitamin D,
  iodine, and often DHA in one pill.

### Eating for fertility and a healthy start

You don't need a special "fertility diet" — just a genuinely balanced one:
vegetables and fruit, whole grains, lean protein, healthy fats, and less
ultra-processed food and sugar. Nutrients worth knowing: **iron** (red meat,
beans, leafy greens), **vitamin D**, **iodine**, and **omega-3 (DHA)**. Keep
**caffeine** moderate (under ~200 mg/day, about one small coffee).

### Weight, movement, sleep, stress

- **Weight:** being significantly under- or over-weight (high or low **BMI**) can
  make conceiving harder and raise pregnancy risks. You don't need to be "ideal" —
  moving toward a healthier range helps.
- **Exercise:** regular moderate activity supports fertility and prepares your
  body; you don't need anything extreme.
- **Sleep & stress:** both affect hormones and wellbeing. Trying to conceive can
  itself be stressful — be kind to yourselves.

### Stop the harms — both of you

- **Smoking & vaping:** harm fertility, sperm, egg, and early development. Quit
  before trying — support is available and worth it.
- **Alcohol:** best to stop; there's no known safe amount once pregnant, and it
  affects fertility for both partners.
- **Recreational drugs:** stop, and be honest with your provider.
- **Environmental/occupational hazards:** ask about exposure to certain
  chemicals, solvents, lead, or radiation at work.

### The partner's part (sperm health)

Since sperm take about **3 months** to mature, the non-carrying partner's choices
now shape the sperm at conception. Helpful: a healthy diet and weight, **no
smoking or excess alcohol**, managing stress, and **avoiding excess heat** to the
testicles (frequent hot tubs/saunas, a laptop directly on the lap). This is a
genuine, shared contribution — not a spectator role.

{: .note }
> **The "start today" shortlist**
> 1. Carrying partner: **start 0.4 mg folic acid** (or a prenatal vitamin).
> 2. **Both:** stop smoking/vaping and alcohol; eat more real food.
> 3. **Both:** move toward a healthier weight with regular activity.
> 4. Non-carrying partner: protect **sperm health** (diet, no smoking, avoid heat).
> These four, begun ~3 months ahead, are most of what "preparing your body" means.

**Q1.** What is folic acid for, what's the usual dose, and why must it start
*before* pregnancy?

**Your answer:**

<details>

It helps prevent <strong>neural-tube defects</strong> (serious defects of the
baby's spine and brain, like spina bifida). The usual dose is <strong>0.4 mg (400
mcg) daily</strong>. It must start <strong>before</strong> conception (ideally 2–3

carrying partner's alone.

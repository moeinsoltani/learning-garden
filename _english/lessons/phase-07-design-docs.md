# Phase 7: Design Docs & Proposals

Phases 4–6 covered short messages and live speaking. This phase moves to *longer-form
writing* — the design docs, proposals, and exec summaries where a lead's ideas get evaluated
and decisions get made. Writing these well is a defining skill: a clear design doc gets your
idea understood and approved; a muddled one buries a good idea and wastes reviewers' time.

The phase covers how to structure a document so it's scannable and complete, writing in plain
language (not impressive-sounding jargon), building paragraphs that flow, distilling an exec
summary for busy leaders, making a proposal persuasive, and writing clear doc comments and
review notes. Built, as always, on your accurate-natural-warm foundation — now at document
length.

The labs are rewrite drills on real doc snippets, plus structure templates you can reuse.

# Phase 2: Trees of Canada

Now the trees themselves — the ones you will actually meet, grouped so their
differences jump out. We start with the **conifers** (the evergreens with
needles), because telling spruce from pine from fir is the single most useful
tree skill in Canada. Then the **maples** — the leaf on the flag and the sap in
the syrup — followed by the other broadleaf trees, the provincial emblems, and
finally the great **forest regions** that stitch all these species into
landscapes.

Every lesson is photo-first: read it beside the pictures, then find the real
thing on your next walk.

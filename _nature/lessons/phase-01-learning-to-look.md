# Phase 1: Learning to Look

Before any names, a habit and a method. Most people walk past a hundred trees
and a dozen birds without seeing one of them — not because they can't, but
because no one ever showed them *what to look at*. This phase fixes that. You'll
learn the handful of features that separate one tree from another and one bird
from another, so that the rest of the course is just filling in a framework you
already own.

Every lesson's lab is a **look-and-identify exercise** — a photo or a described
scene to work through — and the checkpoints check that the *method* has stuck,
not that you've memorised trivia.
